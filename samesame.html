SAME SAME

For this assignment the teachers gave us a sentence “how you feel is how you look”, and we had to choose a textile tradition as a source of inspiration. I picked the American tradition of quilting, for multiple reasons. To make a quilt, you have to combine different fabrics. As this was something I started doing spontaneously the semester before, I thought it was a good starting point to explore this a bit further. The techniques used for quilting spoke to me as well. To manipulate the fabric rather than make one yourself. For a certain kind of quilt, a memory quilt, you have to choose fabrics with a story. Only the way the fabrics were stitched to each other was not ‘meaningful’. I started working on a print, outlining the way my blanked laid upon my bed in the morning, telling the story of the previous night. In a second step, I tried to give the shapes more feeling, by cutting them out of fabric typically used for blankets, filling them, and by that making them into an object. I gave some forms a plaster bath, to enhance their structure and freeze their shape.
